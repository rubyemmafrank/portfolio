Project 4: Open-Source Assistive Technology 10/25

Part 2.1: Review documentation

I found that having both the slide deck and the video tutorial open and switching between them was the most effective use of the documentation. The slides allowed me to move at my own pace, but sometimes I needed a visual demonstration of a specific step. Still, the videos were often not clear or zoomed in enough. The subtle variations across models of the Phoenix hand made the gripper box steps slightly confusing. I would suggest the stinging steps to be further broken down in the slide deck, with close up photos of the specific holes.

Part 2.2: Print & assemble the model

PrusaSlicer

Allie and I set up as many prints as we could as early as possible. We sliced each part, setting the size to 150% and printing with supports. These supports would prove a mistake for some parts later on.

Print fail

The first print of the tensioner pins did not adhere properly and had to be reprintined.

Printing

Color palette

Broken arm cuff

There was a small dent in the first print of our arm cuff after using the heat guns to thermaform it. When Penny and Dr. Krones were trying to smooth it out, it unfortunately snapped down the middle. I had to reprint.

Supports causing issues

I was able to successfully assemble the palm and finger joints after the prints had finished successfully and the cuff was molded. Some of the pins are very slightly loose on the wrist; they could use some glue or a reprint with larger heads on the pins. I strung each finger seperately, insyead of winding the string back and doing two fingers together. So far, this hasn't caused any issues. I think as long as the strings are well tied to the gripperbox, it doesn't matter. Before I did that, I had to assemble the gripperbox itself. When going to screw in the rectangular pins, I realized we had printed them with supports. The supports blocked the hole and could not be removed. We reprinted those pins in the MakerLab, and got MakerLab swipe access together.

Finished palm

After a search for larger nails (I'm assuming becuase of the 50% scale difference, the nails that came in the kit were too small.) we were able to finish assembling the hand! I finished the hand first, on the 21st :) . I propped the hand up on an angle to get the strings tied taught. I did triple knots to keep each finger in place.
